It is not possible to fully experience Vigo without crossing the Rande Bridge by car and contemplating from its height the island of San Simón and the Cíes Islands or the immensity of the Vigo estuary.

Príncipe is Vigo's shopping street. Named in honor of the son of Isabel II, it is the commercial heart of the city with the best shops on the market: Clothing, crafts, shoes, waffles, jewelry and the main Galician fashion brands...

Vigo is sea, and the sea is Vigo. It is not in vain that Vigo is the leading European port in unloading fresh fish. The Vigo fish market is famous for moving a good part of all the fish consumed in Spain and Europe.

Monte de O Castro is the origin of Vigo, where the first settlers settled in the 2nd and 3rd centuries BC. and whose remains are preserved in the Archaeological Site.

The Auditorio Mar de Vigo conference center faces the sea, living up to its name: the building designed by César Portela defies the force of the Atlantic in the heart of the port area to open a gap full of leisure and culture in the most industrial neighborhood of Vigo .

The spectacular Samil beach invites you to spend hours in front of the sea. The Atlantic bathes with its white sands, and the silhouette of the Cíes islands and the island of Toralla clearly show us why the Vigo estuary is different from all of them.

Most Popular Restaurant

La Hacienda de Machado

Located in the bowels of Samil beach, meat lovers will find their particular gastronomic paradise at La Hacienda de Machado. This Vigo restaurant offers diners the unique experience of a Brazilian steakhouse whose specialty is sword meat.

Zebú

Exquisite gastronomy, careful decoration and excellent service are the three maxims of the Zebú project, located at number 18 Arenal Street. This Vigo grill-restaurant specializes in the best products from the sea and land cooked on the grill.

De tapa en Cepa

Creative and traditional cuisine finds a very special fusion among the streets of Vigo, in a restaurant-wine bar known as Detapaencepa and highlighted by the Michelin Guide. This Vigo gastronomic project houses two different spaces under the same roof and concept.

Maruja Limón

The signature cuisine of Rafa Centeno and Inés Abril has become the favorite of many diners who come to Vigo to enjoy the best local cuisine. Awarded with a Michelin star and two Repsol Suns.
